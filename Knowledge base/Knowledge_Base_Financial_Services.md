## 2. Product Knowledge (IUL)

### What is IUL?
Indexed Universal Life insurance is a permanent life insurance policy that:
- Builds cash value linked to market index performance
- Provides death benefit protection
- Offers living benefits while policyholder is alive
- Grows tax-advantaged

### The 11 Benefits of IUL (Blue Screen Presentation Topics)
1. Tax-free growth
2. Tax-free income in retirement
3. Living benefits (access funds while alive)
4. Death benefit protection
5. College funding without student loans
6. Long-term care coverage
7. Protection from market downturns (floor protection)
8. Participation in market gains (with caps)
9. Asset protection from creditors (state dependent)
10. Estate planning tool
11. Flexible premium payments

### Positioning Language
**DO SAY:**
- "Wealth-building vehicle"
- "Financial tool that Fortune 500 executives use"
- "Strategy for tax-free retirement income"
- "College funding account"
- "Living benefits protection"

**DON'T SAY:**
- "Life insurance"
- "Death benefit" (unless specifically asked)
- "Policy" (use "account" or "vehicle")

## 5. Presentation Framework

### Pre-Presentation Checklist

**For Zoom Presentations:**
- [ ] Own Zoom account (not borrowed)
- [ ] Professional backdrop (Amazon)
- [ ] Green laser pointer
- [ ] Blue Screen presentation loaded
- [ ] Quiet environment
- [ ] Good lighting and camera angle
- [ ] Test audio/video 15 minutes early

**For In-Person/Group Presentations:**
- [ ] Arrive 1 HOUR early
- [ ] Professional attire (suit/business)
- [ ] Arrange seating facing TV (NOT kitchen)
- [ ] NO food smells during presentation
- [ ] No eating until AFTER presentation
- [ ] Notebook and pen for attendees
- [ ] Laser pointer ready

### Host Introduction Template
"I want to thank Mrs. [Lucy]. When my husband and I sat down with this lady, she showed us how our children can go to any school in America without student loans. She showed us why owning a house shouldn't stay in your name—it's the biggest mistake. She's impacted lives across North America. I asked if she could share this with my friends, and after [time], she's here today. Please welcome [Lucy]."

### Post-Presentation: The 5 Qualifying Questions

**Opening Statement:**
"Mr./Mrs. [Name], I want to appreciate your time this afternoon. I know you've learned something new today, and I have a couple of questions. Your response will determine if I should proceed or not."

**Question 1: Driver's License**
"Has your driver's license ever been suspended?"
- If NO → proceed
- If YES → note and assess

**Question 2: Driving Violations**
"Have you had any driving violations in the past 12 months?"
- If NO → proceed
- If YES (1 violation): "Let's get you started right now before it goes to 2"
- If 2+ violations: DECLINE

**Question 3: Medications**
*Stop screen sharing when asking this—shows seriousness*
"Are you on any form of medication right now?"
- Write down ALL medications with dosage
- Count prescriptions carefully
- If on blood pressure/cholesterol: "We need to rush this before it goes to a second prescription"
- One medication → rush before it becomes two

**Question 4: COVID History**
"Have you ever been a victim of COVID-19?"
- If YES: "How did you battle COVID?"
- Quarantined at home = GOOD (proceed)
- Oxygen or ventilator = DECLINE

**Question 5: Citizenship**
"Are you a citizen or resident of the United States?"

**Closing Statement:**
"Thank you for your responses. I have good news—there's NO MONEY REQUIRED with this application. It will take 3 days to about 6 months to get approval."

### Post-Approval Announcement Strategy

**CRITICAL: Do NOT announce immediately!**
- If approved in 1-2 days, wait 2-3 days
- Build anticipation
- Then call with excitement:

**Sample Announcement:**
"My sister, I don't know... they cannot believe you... I just submitted your application a couple weeks ago and IT'S APPROVED! Sister, you are anointed!"

## 6. Objection Handling Library

### Objection: "I Have Investments Already"
**Situation:** Prospect has real estate, Bitcoin, stocks, etc.

**Response Framework:**
1. Acknowledge: "When something's not broken, don't fix it."
2. Add: "Keep your Bitcoin. Keep your Tesla stock. Keep your properties."
3. Position: "I'm just showing you something you didn't know about—here's another asset."
4. Reality: "If they're not here, they have NO investment. What happens to those properties?"
5. Protect: "I want to PROTECT what you've built."

### Objection: "I Have Home Equity"
**Situation:** Prospect thinks home equity is enough

**Response:**
"The equity in your home isn't even protected. The home isn't even in a trust. How do you think you'll get that out? How will your kids get it out if you're not here?"

### Objection: "What's In It For You?"
**Situation:** Prospect thinks they're doing YOU a favor

**Response:**
"Let me stop you right there. I spent MY time. I'm licensed across America. What I've seen in the financial field... America is in two parts: those who understand the system and those who work FOR the system. I'm showing you what Fortune 500 companies give their executives—IUL accounts, not 401Ks. I don't have guarantees you'll qualify. It's not even your money. Please stay with your money. Thank you."

*Then walk away.*

### Objection: "I Need To Think About It"
**Response:**
"I completely understand—this is an important decision. Let me ask you this: what specifically would you like to think about? Is it the [benefit mentioned] or [another benefit]? I want to make sure I've addressed all your questions."

### Objection: "I Can't Afford It"
**Response:**
"I hear you. Here's what's interesting—there's NO money required with the application. We first need to see if you qualify. Many people are surprised that the monthly contribution is less than what they spend on [coffee/streaming/etc.]. Let's at least see what options are available for your situation."

### The Sponge Analogy (For Overwhelmed Prospects)
"Everyone washes dishes, right? Everyone uses a sponge. When you put that sponge in water, it can only absorb so much at one time. That's where [prospect] is right now. Give them time, then come back with another 'sponge'—more information. Eventually they absorb it all."

## 13. Red Flags & What to Avoid

### Client Red Flags
- "Can I make my kids the beneficiary?" (wants to benefit from someone else's death)
- Too many questions about YOUR benefits
- Treating it like they're doing YOU a favor
- Asking for guarantees you can't provide

### Agent Mistakes to Avoid
❌ Announcing license immediately after passing exam
❌ Talking about recruitment before closing policy
❌ Using terms like "downline" or "MLM"
❌ Serving food during presentation
❌ Not following up within 24-48 hours
❌ Trying to help everyone (walk away from difficult prospects)
❌ Complaining about studying or challenges (people are watching)
❌ Being envious of others' success

### The Walk-Away Principle
Some prospects will never convert. Know when to say:
"Please stay with your money. Thank you."

Your time and energy are valuable. Invest them in people who are ready.
